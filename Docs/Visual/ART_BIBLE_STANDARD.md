# Art Bible Standard

## Purpose and Authority

An Art Bible is the maintained visual Source of Truth for a project. It narrows ambiguity so assets and scenes can be produced and reviewed coherently. It does not replace the Human Developer's final taste and approval authority, and it does not authorise purchases, production, or implementation.

## Required Sections

1. **Status and change record:** maturity, approved scope, owner, last review, unresolved Human decisions, and change history.
2. **Visual Identity Statement:** one concise rule and supporting principles that resolve ordinary visual ambiguity.
3. **Mood:** emotional targets by major game state, area, or time, with specific atmospheric language.
4. **Shape Language:** dominant/supporting shapes, silhouettes, proportion, edge language, and hierarchy.
5. **Color System:** base palette, accents, semantic roles, temperature relationships, accessibility backups, and controlled exceptions.
6. **Material Language:** surface families, roughness/metallic logic, wear, dirt, repair, age, and consistency rules.
7. **Lighting Hierarchy:** primary/secondary/practical sources, contrast/exposure, navigation, gameplay readability, and profile differences.
8. **Environment Language:** architecture, scale, fronts/backs/services, density, landmarks, boundaries, natural/urban systems, and environmental storytelling.
9. **Character Language:** silhouette, proportion, category distinction, animation/pose, material/detail, and gameplay-distance readability.
10. **Vehicle Language:** class silhouettes, proportion, wear, lights, damage states, and foot/vehicle scale relationship.
11. **UI Language:** typography, shape, iconography, colour, motion, hierarchy, diegetic/screen-space policy, and accessibility.
12. **VFX Language:** shape, timing, colour/energy, density, physical cause, gameplay hierarchy, and performance tiers.
13. **Asset Standards:** formats, scale, texture/detail tiers, LOD/collider/material expectations, naming, provenance, camera distance, and release states, linked to the [Asset Candidate and Integration Standard](../Assets/ASSET_CANDIDATE_AND_INTEGRATION_STANDARD.md).
14. **Use / Avoid:** concrete encouraged and prohibited visual patterns.
15. **Reference Target:** each reference names the exact element to learn from and what must not be copied or inherited.
16. **Hard Style Prohibitions:** focal violations that cause a role `FAIL` unless explicitly excepted by the Human Developer.

## Evidence Standard

Rules should be illustrated where practical with approved references, project captures, palette/material samples, silhouette examples, and gameplay-camera views. A reference board without written `use` and `avoid` interpretation is not an Art Bible.

## Development and Change Process

- Begin with confirmed project direction; mark gaps as Human decisions rather than inventing them.
- Art Director reviews coherence; Technical Artist reviews producibility and budget; UI/UX and other specialists review their sections when relevant.
- Routine clarifications that do not alter approved direction may be maintained inside scope.
- A change to identity, hard prohibitions, product tone, or equivalent taste requires Human approval.
- Record superseded rules; do not silently drift the visual Source of Truth.
- Every visible assignment links the applicable Art Bible version and follows the [Visual Quality Assurance Workflow](Visual_Quality_Assurance_Workflow.md).
